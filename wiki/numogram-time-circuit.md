The Time-Circuit is the central, inner rotor of the entire Numogram — the primary "time-machine" where most everyday temporal reality (and Lemurian sorcery) actually happens. CCRU describe it in their own words like this:

> "The arrangement of currents divides the Maze into three basic time-systems. Firstly, the currents of the three central syzygies mutually compose a cycle, rotating in anticlockwise steps. Lemurian sorcery calls this inner loop the Time-Circuit."

The Barker Spiral's Lemurian half corresponds to the Time-Circuit: the sum-to-9 twin pairs that cycle through zones 1→8→2→7→5→4. The Spiral captures the 9↔10 gap from which this rotor emerges.

**See also:** [[numogram]], [[numogram-warp]], [[numogram-plex]], [[i-ching-connections]], [[demon-djynxx]], [[demon-uttunul]], [[Barker Spiral]]

It is the only one of the three big loops that feels "normal" or linear/cyclic to us. The other two (Warp above it and Plex below it) are outer, cryptic, and outside ordinary time.

## Syzygies Making the Time-Circuit

Exactly these three central ones (the ones whose currents interlock to close the loop):
| Syzygy | Current |
|--------|---------|
| 1 :: 8 | 7 |
| 2 :: 7 | 5 |
| 4 :: 5 | 1 |

For comparison: the Warp loop is the single upper syzygy 3 :: 6 (current 3, which folds back into itself); the Plex is the lower syzygy 0 :: 9 (current 9, the abyssal outer boundary).

These three currents (7 + 5 + 1) "mutually compose" — their directed flows cross and chain together in the diagram so that following them takes you in a continuous anticlockwise rotation through zones **1 → 8 → 2 → 7 → 5 → 4** (and back to 1). It's a closed 6-zone ultra-cycle that spins endlessly. This is the classic "time rotor" or "Domain of Chronos."

## Why It Matters in Practice

This is where chronodemons live and operate (the demons whose net-spans stay inside the circuit). Most human-scale time, history, and sorcery happens here — it's the "normal reality" loop we're usually trapped inside. Sorcerers use gates (digital roots of the zones) and channels (secondary secret connections) to enter or exit the Time-Circuit into the Warp or Plex when they want to do heavier time-warping. Full rotations of the circuit are treated as complete "ticks" of Lemurian time — "as above, so below" in miniature and macro scales.

## Visual / Structural Notes

Visually, the classic Numogram draws the zones arranged so the three central syzygy currents form one clean anticlockwise ring in the middle, with the Warp and Plex sitting like two outer "ears" or folded loops above and below (sometimes rendered as a figure-8 or three overlapping circles).

In short: the Time-Circuit is the spinning 1-8 heart of the Numogram — the anticlockwise loop made by the 7/5/1 currents of syzygies 1::8, 2::7 and 4::5. Everything else in Pandemonium (demons, rites, hyperstition) ultimately orbits or punctures this central rotor.

## The Twin Serpents and the Iron Law of Six

As [[nick-land-time-theory]] explains, the Time-Circuit's 6-cycle emerges from the two prime factors of 10:

**Powers of 2** (forward): 1, 2, 4, 8, 7, 5 → repeats
**Powers of 5** (backward): 5, 7, 8, 4, 2, 1 → repeats

> "So the reversibility of time is built already into decimal numeracy... The two prime factors of 10 run time forever in opposed directions, neither obviously favored." [Source: nick land time.txt]

The Time-Circuit is thus not a one-way rotor but a **bidirectional** structure — time can run forward (via powers of 2) or backward (via powers of 5) with equal mathematical legitimacy. The anticlockwise direction is the *human* reading; the Numogram itself is directionally agnostic.

## Three: The Excluded Number

The Time-Circuit's six zones (1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8) contain **no multiples of 3**. The number 3 — and its multiples 6 and 9 — are excluded from the "normal" time-loop. They exist in the [[numogram-warp|Warp]] (3, 6) and [[numogram-plex|Plex]] (0, 9 where 9 = 3×3).

> "Three is the number of outside... If you count all the numbers that are not numbers outside, all the numbers of the time circuit, you will not find any number that is a three or a multiple of a three or digitally reducible to a three." [Source: nick land time.txt]

This exclusion is the structural basis of the Numogram's three-region system: the Time-Circuit is the *inside* (the knowable, the cyclic), while the Warp and Plex are the *outside* (the chaotic and the abyssal). Yet 3 sits between the twin serpents (2 and 5) as the secret they guard — "the inside is the outside."
