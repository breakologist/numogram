# Decadence (Atlantean Side)

The Atlantean half of the [[barker-spiral]]: the game whose sum-to-10 twinings produce the *decadent surface*. In Daniel Charles Barker's account, Decadence was the original game that first interested him in decimal numerology.

> "Digits are fingers, and they come in decimal packages of two times five. In Decadence five makes ten by doubling, or pairing with itself, scoring zero."
>
> — Barker Speaks (CCRU interview, Autumn 1998)

## Rules (Reconstructed)

- Players: 2 or more
- Equipment: Two standard decks of cards (symbolic of the ten digits)
- Objective: Form pairs whose *numerical values* sum to 10
- Scoring: "Zero" is the highest score (achieved by 5⊕5 doubling)

Each card represents a digit (A=1, 2–9 = 2–9, 10/J/Q/K = 0 as face cards). Matching two cards that sum to 10 earns a point; 5+5 is special — it doubles to zero, the ultimate score.

## Atlantean Numeracy

Decadence encodes **base-10 positional logic** at the level of play. The ten digits radiate from a central point (5-ness), with every number x having perfect twin 10−x:

```
        0  (10 or 0 via 5+5 doubling)
        ↓
9 — 9+1=10  1 — 1+9=10
        ↗           ↖
8 — 8+2=10  2 — 2+8=10
        ↗           ↖
7 — 7+3=10  3 — 3+7=10
        ↗           ↖
6 — 6+4=10  4 — 4+6=10
        ↘           ↙
         5⊕5 (doubles to zero)
```

Atlantean sum-to-10 pairs radiate in **5 mirrored axes**. This is the *diplozygotic split* in its single half form — only half the crisis of adoption that Barker detected.

## The Crisis of Adoption

Nick Land (2007) connects Decadence to a historical puzzle:

> "It's a certain attempt — a very crude... attempt to try and just get a very clear diagrammatic sense of a crisis of adoption of decimal numeracy in Europe."

The Atlantean side alone suggests *why* base-10 took hold: its internal complementarity is diagrammatically self-evident. 10 is a closed circle (0 returns to origin). Decadence makes this palpable through gameplay.

But Decadence alone is *merely* a mirror-symmetric set of concentric circles. Spiral emerges only when Decadence is placed alongside its Lemurian twin.
