# Body Mapping — The Hands Are the Numogram

A tactile method for holding the [[numogram]] in the body. Steeple your fingers and look through your hands.

Source: Aamodt, *Unleashing the Numogram*.

## The Mapping

| Fingers | Syzygy | Region |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Middle fingers | 3 :: 6 | [[numogram-warp\|Warp]] |
| Index fingers | 2 :: 7 | Time-Circuit |
| Ring fingers | 5 :: 4 | Time-Circuit |
| Pinkies | 1 :: 8 | Time-Circuit |
| Thumbs | 9 :: 0 | [[numogram-plex\|Plex]] |

## Usage

Steeple your fingers (tips touching, palms apart). Look through the gap between your hands.

- Your **middle fingers** are the Warp — 3 and 6, the chaotic vortex. They're in the centre of the steeple, the most prominent fingers. The chaos is always at the heart of the system.
- Your **thumbs** are the Plex — 9 and 0, the abyss. They're at the edges, disconnected from the main body of the steeple. The abyss brackets the whole system.
- The **Time-Circuit** wraps through your remaining fingers: 1→8 (pinkies), 2→7 (index), 5→4 (ring). The circuit of ordinary time passes through the periphery.

## Divinatory Application

When thinking about a zone, touch the corresponding finger. Zone 3 concerns? Touch the left middle finger. Plex questions? Work the thumbs. The tactile anchor bypasses abstraction and places the querent *inside* the system.

## Roguelike Application

The body map is the minimap. The player's hands are always available. When lost in the dungeon, steeple the fingers: middle is the centre (Warp), thumbs are the edges (Plex), the circuit runs through everything else.
