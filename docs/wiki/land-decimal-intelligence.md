## The Three Cipher Methods

### 1. Alphanumeric Cabala (AQ)

The primary method. Take any word/phrase → run through a cipher (ciphers.news) → get a number → digitally reduce → slot onto the numogram.

> *"Any rational number is digitally reducible. So it can literally be anything. Crunch it down. If it's rational, it's digitally reducible. Any rational number ultimately fits into a slot on the numogram."*

Key insight: "Any number at all is just indexing that" — the numogram as a universal index for all linguistic content.

### 2. Plexenomy

Prime factor compression. Express numbers purely by their prime factor relations — **baseless**, independent of decimal or any base. The clearest expression of Euclid's Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic in notational form.

> *"A way of compressing numbers down into a form that is actually baseless. It's just the clearest expression of Euclid's fundamental theorem of arithmetic in notational form."*

### 3. Liquid Decoglyphics

Analysis of the **form** of numerals themselves — the 7-segment display of digital clocks and calculators. The number 8 fills all segments; other numerals are subsets. This completely formalizes the semiotic form of all 10 decimal numerals.

> *"If you look at a calculator or a digital watch... there's this space consisting of eight bars. The number eight completely fills out. The zero is just that eight with the central bar missing."*

### Bonus: Factorial Reduction

Not a full method but a noted alternative to digital reduction:
1. Take a number, extract all prime factors
2. Add them together (instead of multiplying)
3. If result is composite, factorize again
4. Recurse until you reach a prime or single digit

## 360 — The Number of the System

The sum of all syzygy net-spans: **90 + 81 + 72 + 63 + 54 = 360**.

| Syzygy | Net-Span | Value | Region |
|--------|----------|-------|--------|
| 0::9 | 90 | 90 | Plex (Outside) |
| 1::8 | 81 | 81 | Time-Circuit |
| 2::7 | 72 | 72 | Time-Circuit |
| 3::6 | 63 | 63 | Warp (Outside) |
| 4::5 | 54 | 54 | Time-Circuit |

**The Split:**
- **153** = Outside (90 + 63) — Warp + Plex, the zones the classifier can't reach
- **207** = Time Circuit (81 + 72 + 54) — the "normal" rotor where the classifier works

> *"207 is the number of the time circuit. 153 is the number outside. 153 + 207 = 360 which is the number of the whole system."*

**360 AQ resonance:** "Decimal Labyrinth" = 360. "Hermetic cosmogyny" = 153 + 207 = 360.

### Connection to Classifier Work

The 153/207 split maps directly to our classifier diversity problem:
- **207** = Time-Circuit zones {1,2,4,5,7,8} — the classifier's home territory (96.4% when locked)
- **153** = Warp+Plex zones {0,3,6,9} — the classifier's blind spot (0% accuracy for Z0, Z8, Z9 on diverse params)

The classifier IS the 207. The diversity expansion is the quest to reach the 153.

## 89 Paths — The Book of Paths

Three legitimate moves on the numogram:

| Move | Operation | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Cross a syzygy** | Move to partner zone | Z1 → Z8 |
| **Follow a current** | Move to tractor zone | Z8 → Z7 (via current 7) |
| **Take a gate** | Use triangular gate from zone | Z8 → Z9 (via Gt-36) |

> *"Those are the only three operations. And then if you are restricted to those three operations, where can you get? You can obviously ultimately get everywhere."*

Total unique paths: **89**.

**89 is how you pass from inside to outside** — through Gate 36 (Z8→Z9, the plunge). The 36th triangular number is 666. Land calls this "the terminal vector."

### Connection to Zone-Over-Time

Our proposed zone-over-time classifier output (a track is a trajectory, not a point) is exactly a path through the 89. A song that moves Z4→Z2→Z7→Z3 has taken: cross syzygy (Z4→Z5? no, that's not the path), follow current? Actually Z4→Z2 is a gate or direct jump... 

The 89 paths give us a **bounded vocabulary** for describing musical zone trajectories. A classifier that reports "this track followed path #47" is speaking the numogram's native language.
