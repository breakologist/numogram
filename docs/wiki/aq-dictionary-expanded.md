## Mod-9 Heresy (@cybermonist)

- **D** = set of ordered pairs (m, n) mod 9, m+n≡0. This is the "syzygy group" — every pair is a valid syzygy because they sum to the identity element (0).
- **(D, +)** is an abelian group. The highlighted subgroups <(1,8)> and <(3,6)> are the classic pairs that sum to 0 mod 9 (1+8=9≡0, 3+6=9≡0). These generate the structure.
- **Δ : D → D** (green box) is an automorphism defined by (m, n) ↦ (m − n, n − m). This is the "current generator" — it takes any syzygy and produces the "difference" in both directions. Because we work mod 9, subtraction wraps around beautifully.
- **φ_m and φ_n** (red and blue boxes) are homomorphisms that map any pair (m, n) to the triangular currents associated with its first or second component. These are the precise algebraic versions of the "Phi" mappings in the traditional Numogram.
- **Why this is a "heretical abomination"**: By working entirely in mod 9, the number 9 disappears (9 ≡ 0). The entire Numogram becomes a clean, finite abelian group with perfect symmetry and inverses. This breaks the traditional decimal/Pythagorean emphasis on 9 as a special number, but gains enormous algebraic elegance and clarity. @xenocosmography called it "the most beautiful heretical abomination I've yet seen."
