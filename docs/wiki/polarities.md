# Polarities

Each [[numogram]] zone has a positive (+) or negative (−) polarity — an aspect that determines its qualitative character within a syzygy.

Source: Aamodt, *Unleashing the Numogram*, drawing on CCRU descriptions.

## Polarity Table

| Zone | Polarity | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 0 | − | Dark aspect of Foundation ("deep past"). Protocosmic abyss anticipating primal reality, fusing indissociably into the ultimate gulfs of chaotic unbeing. |
| 1 | + | Light aspect of Anamnesis ("memories and dreams"). Enduring ideas, historical time, and remembrance (recall). |
| 2 | − | Dark aspect of Horizon ("boundaries and limits"). |
| 3 | + | Light aspect of Process ("becoming and transformation"). |
| 4 | − | Dark aspect of Gate ("thresholds and crossings"). |
| 5 | + | Light aspect of Current ("flow and direction"). |
| 6 | − | Dark aspect of Warp ("chaos and acceleration"). |
| 7 | + | Light aspect of Rise ("ascent and elevation"). |
| 8 | − | Dark aspect of Hold ("stasis and preservation"). |
| 9 | + | Light aspect of Plex ("terminal involution and completion"). |

## Interpretation

Within each syzygy, one pole is positive and one is negative. The syzygy 3::6 pairs the light aspect of Process (+3) with the dark aspect of Warp (−6). The syzygy 0::9 pairs the dark abyss (−0) with the light completion (+9).

The positive zones (1, 3, 5, 7, 9) are all odd. The negative zones (0, 2, 4, 6, 8) are all even. The numogram has a built-in even/odd, dark/light binary that runs through its entire structure.

The Time-Circuit alternates: +1 → −8 → −2 → +7 → +5 → −4 → (back to +1). The polarity flips at each step of the rotor.

## Application: Dungeon Design

Positive zones are "open" rooms — rewards, choices, expansions. Negative zones are "closed" rooms — traps, locks, compressions. The alternation of polarity within the Time-Circuit creates a rhythmic tension: open-close-open-close, never two of the same in a row.
