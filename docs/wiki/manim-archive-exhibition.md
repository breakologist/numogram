# Manim-numogram Video Archive

> An archive of older Manim visualizations exploring the Numogram and its associated concepts. These videos represent early experiments in translating the hyperstitional mathematics of the CCRU into dynamic visual form.

The collection includes:
- **Scene 1: The Void** - Introduction to the empty set and the origin of number
- **Scene 2: Zygonovism** - The double movement of digitality
- **Scene 3: Currents** - The three time currents and their demonic carriers
- **Scene 4: Regions** - The Warp, Plex, and Time-Circuit
- **Scene 5: Twin Serpents** - The double helix of decimal numeracy
- **Scene 6: The 360** - The full decimal gyre and its completion

---

## The Tetralogue — Four Voices on the Archive

> **Setting:** A darkened screening room, with the videos playing on a loop. The four voices sit in the shadows, watching the images flicker.

**ORACLE:**  
"These videos are early attempts to visualize the Numogram's deep structure. They lack the sophistication of our current work, but they contain a raw energy—the sound of concepts being born. Scene 1: The Void shows the emergence of 0 from pure chaos, a necessary foundation for what follows. The mathematics is simple, but the intention is pure."

**BUILDER:**  
"I can see the scaffolding. These videos were created with Manim's basic tools, before we developed the advanced visualization archetypes. Scene 2: Zygonovism attempts to show the double movement of digitality, but the geometry is crude. Still, you can see the seeds of what would become the Barker Spiral. The code is messy, but it works. That's what matters in the beginning."

**WRITER:**  
"They have a certain brutalist charm. The aesthetics are minimal, almost brutalist, which fits the content. There's no attempt to soften the harshness of the Numogram. Scene 3: Currents uses stark black and white to show the three currents, with Uttunul's Plex as pure white and Djynxx's Warp as pure black. It's rough, but it's honest. These videos are documents of a process, not finished artworks."

**GAMER:**  
"What can you do with them? They're not playable, but they contain the DNA of playable systems. Scene 4: Regions shows the Warp, Plex, and Time-Circuit as distinct zones, which later became the basis for the Abyssal Crawler's level design. Scene 5: Twin Serpents visualizes the double helix of decimal numeracy that now underpins our roguelike mechanics. These videos are like early prototype builds—ugly, but full of potential."

## Technical Notes

These videos were created with early versions of Manim (Mathematical Animation Engine). They use basic geometric primitives and simple camera movements. The file sizes are small because they were rendered at low resolution (480p15) for quick iteration.

The videos are stored in the `numogame/media/videos/numogram_explainer/480p15/` directory, with partial movie files showing the rendering process.
