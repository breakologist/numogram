### Zone 2 - Separation - dt - ♀
#### Ruins - Tropic (from 1991's Early Works) 
A proggy riff fest.  Appropriately, there are two players, one bassist and one drummer/vocalist.  Sounds like it was recorded via microphone in a physical space to my ear, has a more raw sound.  The bass is nicely overdriven, fittingly fiery and noisy,  and the drums/vocals tightly woven rhythmically and soundwise with the bass, the cymbals too link this to the noise waveform.  The track cuts between groovier and more frantic or staccato sections ( the structure is something like: ABCD-ABCD-E-AD) , but always played tightly despite the apparent chaos.  Pretty typical in character for a Ruins track, the vocals (singing, but more like speaking, an invented or glossolaic language, again typical for Ruins) bubble and babble over the rhythms of the instruments , a bit like a rollercoaster along a track. dt feels an appropriate particle the bass could be d and the drums t. 

#### Prelude - Wedding 
Nobuo Uematsu's Final Fantasy prelude, heard throughout the series. Here starting with some chiming bells, then the ascending and descending arpeggio motif played by a harp (possibly synthesised), then comes a church organ (perhaps the reason for the association with fire/noise/density) , playing the slower chords and making more complex the tone , the piece rises slowly, crescendos and plateaus then slowly fades. 

#### Toshio Tasaki - Epilogue 1 - From Devil Summoner: Soul Hackers
Two chords are slowly played on a (synthesised) piano, with some echo/delay and reverb. A soft melodic synth appears to carry the track along, and the chords then diversify and a slight tension appears, then disappears, then the track fades.  Quite a peaceful one, maybe fitting to Zone 4 besides 2


### Zone 6 - Abstraction - tch - ♄
#### Madlib Medicine Show 3: Beat Konducta In Africa - Raw Introduction To Afreaka
Made entirely from various samples (maybe two sources), phased and echoed to varying degrees, some recorded on tape perhaps. Mostly speech samples revolving around descriptions of Africa, from a CIA factbook or a holiday rep sort of perspective fading in and out "This is Africa, land of *all* black people" sounds like it was from the 70s.  The main music comes from a slowed sample of a softish funk groove with dominant organ and bass, guitar and some clipped vocals (the band is Witch - and the track is Introduction, from 2007).  
#### Noriyuki Asakura - Faraway (Ending Theme) - From Tenchu Stealth Assassins (1998)
Beginning with soft, plaintive breathy synths, a melody building, then synth bass, distant drums, then takes off into the main line a drumkit joins the more soft-thunder drums, bass, piano and guitar arrive, with strings playing  a slightly mysterious yet melancholic triumphant line, at times the track fades, leaving softly thundering drums and chanting vocals in one interlude and more instrumental elaborations later on, with call-backs to songs from earlier in the soundtrack album. I'd guess some sort of eastern scale. Feels rather polished and accomplished overall, a sophisticated sounding production somehow, yet in its climax does manage a sort of subdued exuberance before arriving at a more solemn close.  For a 5 minute track I should have more to say, but have enjoyed this album and the game it is from, for some years now.
#### Yuka Kitamura - 淤加美の一族 - (the Okami Clan)
Though short, this track is instantly dramatic, but quietly so , intense and brooding yet not too bombastic, an elegant sort of surge or movement about it also, dense from the start.  The melodic aspect held by strings droning out long sliding chords and flutes swirling in the verse alongside large warlike drums, then give way to a time of more space and slowed drums and sinister bass while the strings and flute coalesce and cohere in a way tricky to describe , pleasantly and rather menacing. Then the first section is taken up again, and fades out.  From a game I've yet to play (Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice, also features Noriyuki Asakura on other tracks - and itself as a game something of a spiritual successor to the Tenchu series).  It is hard to say where it belongs, but in the mind's eye there is fire and swirling fabrics, it's easy to imagine this fitting a boss fight.  


### Zone 4 - Gate - skr - ♂
### Shoji Meguro - Misfortune - From Raidou Kuzunoha 2
A short dramatic building track , begins quietly yet urgently with low/high strings and occasional snare drum, punctuated by brass. Culminates in a repeating string motif rising in pitch and intensity, while accompanied by a brass instrument (perhaps trombone) plaintively sounds a sort of warning. The piece then descends, cascades in tone and in time to a moment that sounds like a trap might have been strung or fittingly a gate has slammed shut. Perhaps also a foe to be faced.  Feels very Shoji Meguro. 

### Derek & The Ruins - Manugan Melpp
the same Ruins as above, with Derek. Sounds like it's just guitar and effects with light percussion (small cymbals or bells, almost like wind chimes or maybe glockenspiel, something metal for sure). A lot of space, a high chord to start , a delay pedal feedback lightly, maybe a spring-reverb,  the bells strike at different places in the stereo field, while the guitar pensively plays harmonics and picks at small chords. It's fairly neutral or non-commital in tone, it doesn't feel like anything's being asked or answered overtly, feels like an interlude, but loops well. High and tinkly, spacious and slowly sparkly. 
#### Akachochin Shiraishi - from Persona 2 罰 Eternal Punishment
Synthesised strings (plucked and bowed) and horns alongside simple drums with pleasantly flanged hi-hats, an organ swells quietly in the background. Slightly triumphant yet subdued, a melodica/accordian sounding line (also calls back to a tune from the first Persona 2 game : Innocent Sin) a motif plays a couple times, then an ascending tension , lead by the accordion, plateaus and returns to the main motif, while the hi-hat gets more lively, there's a little breakdown where a plucked string appears and takes over, then the drums go a bit breakbeat while the delayed plucked string goes for a solo over the top, eventually the horns/strings bring us back to where we started, then fade.  Quite good fun for a track that possibly came from a shop or dialogue section, i would guess.  There's a quiet chirpiness and a bit of humour about it somehow.

### Zone 9 - Plex - tn - ⯓
#### Benedicamus domino - "let us bless the Lord" or something like this - By Anonymous 4
A latin liturgical piece, performed by the american vocal group Anonymous 4. Four female voices circulate this simple text and tones with a pleasant roomy reverb.  Fitting it should be associated with the outside. Amusing the hellish/Cthulhu connotations of zone 9 in this context, perhaps fitting too considering YHVH etc.
