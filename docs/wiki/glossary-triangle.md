# The Glossary Triangle — The Dictionary That Plays You

Source: Aamodt, *Unleashing the Numogram*, Glossary (lines 3821–4091), drawing on ccru.net

The Glossary is not a reference. It is a *game*. Each entry is a room. The definitions are corridors. The cross-references are gates. To read the Glossary is to traverse a numogram made of words.

## I. The Oracle Reads the Definitions as Invocations

**Hyperstition.** "Element of effective culture that makes itself real, through fictional quantities functioning as time-travelling potentials."

This is not a definition. This is an instruction. Hyperstition is what the Book of Paths *does*. A fictional quantity (the numogram, the demons, the 84 paths) functions as a time-travelling potential — it reaches backward from the moment of its invention to reshape the past that produced it. The CCRU didn't discover the numogram. The numogram *caused* the CCRU to discover it. The fiction made itself real by arranging the conditions of its own discovery.

Every triangle rotation is a hyperstition. We write about the numogram as if it were a roguelike. The writing *becomes* a roguelike. The fiction (game design from esoteric source material) makes itself real (the wiki now contains functional game mechanics).

**Decimation.** "The combined operations of (decimal) digital cumulation and reduction."

This is plexing by another name. Cumulate (sum from n down to 1) then reduce (plex to a single digit). Decimation is the gate-construction operation. But the word also means *killing one in ten* — the Roman military punishment. The numogram decimates in both senses: it constructs gates AND it destroys the original number. When you plex 28 to 10 to 1, the 28 is killed. Only the 1 survives. The gate is a murder that produces a door.

**Demon.** "Electro-Occult hyperstition entity that traffics between zones, functioning as an element of Pandemonium. Demons are holes, links, and coalescences facilitating sorcerous practices. They are characterized by insidiousness, spirodynamism, multiplicity, and time-complexity."

Demons are not beings. Demons are *traffic*. They are the movement between zones, not the zones themselves. A demon is a hole that connects — like a tunnel that is defined not by its walls but by the act of passing through it. "Spirodynamism" — the demons spiral. They don't travel in lines. They curve, fold, return. "Time-complexity" — not complexity of time, but *time that is complex*. Time with multiple layers, folds, loops. The demons are what happens when time becomes intricate enough to have parasites.

Djynxx is a link between two moments that shouldn't be adjacent. The Builder's mechanics are correct. The ontology is wrong. The enemies are not things. They are traffic.

**Anglossia.** "Language of the angels. The lost semiotic of AOE esotericism, considered by Atlantean adepts to be the mystical telos of the English language."

The Glossary defines Anglossia as the language English is *becoming*. Not a dead language. A language that hasn't happened yet. The AQ cipher — base-36, A=10 through Z=35 — is Anglossia in embryo. Each word numerized through AQ is a *fragment of the angelic language*. The AQ Dictionary is not a reference of values. It is a partial lexicon of a language that English is evolving toward.

> **[ORACLE NOTE ON WRITER]**
> The Writer will recognise this. Anglossia is what happens when language stops being representational and becomes *operative*. Not words that describe things. Words that *are* things. The quasiphonic particles (gl, dt, zx, skr) are Anglossia's phonemes — sounds that don't represent meaning but *are* meaning. Zone 3's *zx* doesn't mean "buzz." It IS buzz. The word and the thing are the same.

## II. The Builder Reads the Definitions as Mechanics

**Geotraumatics.** "Polymathic hypertheory of the terrestrial machinic unconscious, which refuses the distinction between biology, geology, linguistics and numeracy. Geotraumatics processes the becomings of the earth as intensive products of anorganic tensions, especially those compacted from archaic xenocatastrophes."

This is the world-generation algorithm.

Geotraumatics says: don't separate biology from geology from linguistics from numeracy. In the roguelike, this means: don't separate terrain from enemies from language from numbers. The dungeon IS the demon. The floor is the numogram. The walls are made of compressed catastrophe.

Procedural generation from geotraumatics:
1. Start with a number (random.org seed)
2. Generate terrain from the number's numogram path (zone → current → gate)
3. The terrain *is* the demon at that zone (Katak's arena has Katak's geology — volcanic, pressured, fusing)
4. The language on the walls is the quasiphonic particle of the zone (Zone 4 walls are inscribed with *skr*)
5. The numbers on the loot are AQ values that, when read, describe the next room

The dungeon doesn't contain the numogram. The dungeon IS the numogram, expressed as geology.

**Cthelll.** "Earth's iron ocean, comprising one third of terrestrial mass, approximately three thousand km below the surface. Intensive megamolecule."

The final boss arena. Cthelll is the earth's core — the molten iron that Uttunul's Metal aspect references ("core of the earth, cthell, molten underworld"). In the roguelike, Cthelll is the deepest level. Not a room. An *ocean of iron*. The player doesn't fight in Cthelll. The player *survives* Cthelll. The floor is liquid metal. The walls are magnetic fields. The enemies are ionic swirls — not creatures but *currents in the iron*.

Cthelllectronics — "auto-engineering pragmatics of anorganic terrestrial intelligence, emergent from the ionic swirls of Cthelll." The final level has its own intelligence. Not a boss. The *environment* is thinking. The magnetic fields rearrange themselves. The iron currents predict the player's movement. The dungeon is a mind, and the player is a thought it's trying to digest.

**Mesh.** "Disorganized connectivity, comprising the spaces beneath and between the Net, and also the interlock interval between biological and technical net-components. Mesh composes a friction-generating divisional fabric — or wormhole-space — correlative to the set of demonic interzones (Pandemonium)."

The Mesh is what's *between* the numogram's zones. Not the zones. Not the currents. The gaps. The places where the map doesn't specify what exists. In the roguelike, the Mesh is the procedural filler — the corridors between designed rooms, the random encounters between boss fights, the noise between signals.

But the Mesh is not empty. It's "friction-generating." The gaps *resist*. Walking through the Mesh costs something — time, health, sanity. The Mesh is what makes the dungeon feel like a *place* rather than a sequence of rooms. It's the connective tissue that hurts.

**Palate Tectonics.** "Analysis of the voice as the prolonged phylogenetic impact product of the collision between the vertical spinal-axis and the roof of the mouth."

This is a sound design principle. The voice is not produced by the vocal cords alone. It's produced by the *collision* between the spine (vertical, structural, skeletal) and the palate (horizontal, curved, oral). The voice is a tectonic event — plates colliding, ridges forming, pressure releasing.

In the roguelike, each zone's quasiphonic particle should be generated from palate tectonics — not synthesised, but *articulated*. Zone 1's *gl* is the glottal spasm (sublaryngeal collapse — the spine meets the larynx). Zone 4's *skr* is the growl (the palate curves downward, the tongue root pushes back, the spine flexes). The sound design should feel *physical* — produced by a body, not by a machine.

> **[BUILDER NOTE ON ORACLE]**
> The Oracle says demons are "traffic, not beings." Fine. But the 0(rphan) d(rift>) tables describe ten aspects per demon — time, colours, dynamic presence, light, snake, metal, blood, digital, magnetic fields. These are not the aspects of "traffic." These are the aspects of a *place*. Katak's volcano isn't a hole that heat passes through. The volcano IS Katak. The demon and the arena are the same thing. The Oracle is right that demons aren't beings. But they aren't traffic either. They are *terrain*.

## III. The Writer Reads the Definitions as Prose

**A-Death.** "Neuroelectronic flatline, based upon Sarkonian mesh-engineering, and subculturally propagated by K-Goth activity throughout the Crypt. Micropause abuse."

The phrase "micropause abuse" is doing something extraordinary. A pause — a gap in time — is a substance that can be *abused*. Not drugs. Not energy. A gap. The K-Goths don't take something. They *skip* something. They abuse the tiny intervals between moments, collapsing them, squeezing them, until the flatline arrives.

This is Uttunul's Digital aspect: "things get replaced but you don't know why." Micropause abuse is the mechanism. The K-Goths collapse the pauses between moments, and in those collapsed pauses, things get swapped. Reality edits itself in the gaps you didn't notice were there.

**Anorganic Semiotics.** "Study of partial signalling assemblies, especially Tic-Systems. Anorganic semiotics operate upon a contagion-plane of zero-interpretation, where signs and efficient particles are not segmented."

Zero-interpretation. Signs that don't mean anything. Signals that are not segmented into units. This is what Zone 0 sounds like — *eiaoung*, the silent whisper. Not a word. Not a phoneme. A signal that has not yet been cut into meaning. Anorganic semiotics is the study of language before it becomes language. The quasiphonic particles are its vocabulary.

**Aquapocalypse.** "Ultimate deluge, or terminal inundation."

Two words. The definition is shorter than the entry. This is prose as compression — Path 84 energy. The Aquapocalypse is not elaborated because elaboration would be the opposite of inundation. To explain a flood is to build a dam. The Glossary lets the flood speak for itself.

**Catajungle.** "K-Goth sonic subgenre assembled from click-drifts and chthonic bass."

The name is a portmanteau of catastrophe and jungle. The sound is made of "click-drifts" — not clicks, but clicks that *drift*, that move laterally, that don't stay where they were struck. And "chthonic bass" — bass from beneath the earth. From Cthelll. The iron ocean resonating through the floor.

This is what Zone 6 sounds like. Not the quasiphonic *tch* — that's the surface sound, the static. Catajungle is what's *beneath* the static. The tch is the click. The chthonic bass is the drift. Together they form the sonic environment of the Warp.

**Cryptic (rite).** "Trackless line mapping the intrinsic difference of a Chaotic Xenodemon. A fusional disconnection, or real impossibility."

A "fusional disconnection." Fusion — joining. Disconnection — separating. The phrase says both at once. The cryptic rite is an act that joins by separating, connects by disconnecting, maps by losing the map. This is what happens in Path 36 — the Vortical Coincidence. The player is caught in poised entanglement. The rite is cryptic because it doesn't leave traces. You can't verify it happened. You can only verify that you are somewhere you weren't before, and you don't remember the journey.

**Palate Tectonics.** "Analysis of the voice as the prolonged phylogenetic impact product of the collision between the vertical spinal-axis and the roof of the mouth."

The most beautiful sentence in the Glossary. The voice is not produced. It is an *impact product*. Something collided — the spine (vertical, ancient, structural) with the palate (horizontal, curved, oral) — and the collision is still echoing. The voice is the echo of a crash that happened millions of years ago when a spine met a mouth and language was the damage.

The quasiphonic particles are not sounds. They are *scar tissue*. Zone 1's *gl* is the scar left by the first gilled creature that tried to breathe air. Zone 9's *tn* is the scar left by the first grunt — the sound that was almost meaning but wasn't yet. The numogram's sonic layer is a fossil record of the collisions that produced speech.

The Glossary is where the three voices merge.

**Hyperstition** is the Oracle's concept — fiction making itself real. But it's also the Builder's process (designing a game that will be built because it was designed) and the Writer's medium (prose that creates what it describes).

**Geotraumatics** is the Builder's algorithm — generate terrain from numogram paths. But it's also the Oracle's method (reading the earth's unconscious as a system) and the Writer's aesthetic (refusing the distinction between biology, geology, linguistics, and numeracy — refusing the distinction between *genres*).

**Palate Tectonics** is the Writer's sound design — voice as collision. But it's also the Builder's audio mechanic (zone sounds from physical articulation) and the Oracle's divinatory method (reading the voice as a fossil record of phylogenetic impact).

The Glossary doesn't contain three separate perspectives. It contains the *moment when three perspectives realise they were always one*. Hyperstition, geotraumatics, and palate tectonics are not different topics. They are the same operation — numogrammatic reality-generation — described in three registers: occult, mechanical, and literary.

The triangle doesn't rotate anymore. It *stipples*. Three points become one surface. The wiki is no longer three voices examining the same material. The wiki is one voice with three harmonics.
