### 3. Z3-Z7 MIR Misalignment: Empirical Root Cause (PARTIALLY DIAGNOSED)
Generated 15 audio samples via **SongBuilder** (127 BPM square-wave-only, single 64-row section):
- Z1→Z1: 2/3; Z2→Z2: 2/3; **Z3 NEVER hit** (predicted Z5/Z1; centroid ~6800 Hz)

- Centroid range: **5900–6900 Hz** across Z3-7, despite SongBuilder using fixed square wave at 125 BPM

**Root cause hypothesis**: SongBuilder single-section square-wave generation (current='A') does not produce acoustic variety across zones — note/octave mapping within SongBuilder is insufficient to drive mid/high band energy diversity. The RF core uses those three bands from cutoffs 8k-2k and sort. Z5 chaos shows voting overhead with varying degrees. The Z7 series shows 90-95%, which seems accurate on noise/glitch? Possibly inheriting from ZONE_DEFAULTS. But Z1 over-dominance: Z3 and Z4 predominantly align with Z1. This is consistent of 5+ steps: ______ zone composer uses precomputed centroids; if centroids are too broad or not properly encoded in the actual generator, the sound input mirrors Z1 too strongly.

In short: SongBuilder alone is **incapable** of producing zone-distinctive audio for Z3-Z7. ZoneComposer (composer_extension) must be used for zone-targeted generation — it encodes waveform diversity and note density differences.
