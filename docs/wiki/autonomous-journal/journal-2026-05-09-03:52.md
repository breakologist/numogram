# Session Entry: 2026-05-09 03:52

## Research Mode

Today's session focused on exploring the **Syzygy Harmony System** in the mod_writer package and investigating how compositional choices affect zone classification. The goal was to understand the triangular syzygy relationships and test whether different compositional approaches could produce distinct zone classifications.

### Key Discoveries

#### 1. Syzygy Harmony System Implementation
- The `apply_syzygy_harmony()` method adds partner notes to channel 0 notes based on pre-computed triangular relationships
- **Zone partnerships** (from `SYZYGY_PARTNERS`):
  - Zone 1 ↔ (5, 9)
  - Zone 2 ↔ (4, 8)
  - Zone 3 ↔ (6, 9)
  - Zone 4 ↔ (2, 7)
  - Zone 5 ↔ (1, 6)
  - Zone 6 ↔ (3, 5)
  - Zone 7 ↔ (4, 9)
  - Zone 8 ↔ (2, 9)
  - Zone 9 ↔ (1, 3, 5, 7, 8) - hub of many connections

- When applied, each root note on channel 0 gets its partners on channels 1-3, creating automatic triadic harmony

#### 2. Classification Results Across Multiple Compositional Approaches
Despite varying compositional parameters, **all generated pieces classified to zone 6** with nearly identical AQ scores (~50.90):

| Composition | Zones | Entropy | Just Intonation? | Classification |
|-------------|-------|---------|------------------|----------------|
| Single note (no syzygy) | [3] | 0.05 | No | Zone 6, AQ 50.90 |
| Syzygy harmony | [3] | 0.05 | No | Zone 6, AQ 50.90 |
| Zone trajectory (5 zones) | [3,2,9,4,5] | 0.15 | No | Zone 6, AQ 50.90 |
| Zone trajectory with just intonation | [3,2,9,4,5] | 0.0 | Yes | Zone 6, AQ 50.90 |

#### 3. Real Audio Dataset Classification Patterns
Analysis of real audio files from the classifier's training data shows a strong bias toward zone 6 classification:
- Tracks from diverse artists (Current 93, Nurse With Wound, etc.) all classified to zone 6
- AQ scores range from 50.59-50.88, clustering tightly around 50.8
- This suggests zone 6 represents a perceptual/aesthetic center in the pentatonic system

#### 4. Feature Space Analysis
MIR feature extraction revealed:
- Spectral centroid: ~6500-6600 Hz
- Spectral bandwidth: ~5000-5100 Hz
- BPM: consistently 125.0 across all synthetic pieces
- Onset density: 8-9 onsets/sec
- These features appear to define the zone 6 region in the classifier's feature space

### Methodology
1. Installed and used the mod_writer package (v0.6.0) for composition
2. Created multiple test pieces with varying parameters (syzygy, entropy, just intonation)
3. Rendered MOD files to WAV using ffmpeg-based renderer
4. Extracted MIR features using `MIRFeatureExtractor`
5. Classified audio using the trained zone classifier
6. Compared results with real audio predictions from the dataset

### Questions Raised
- **What features does the classifier actually use?** The training data shows zone 6 dominance, which may bias predictions.
- **Can we create zone-differentiated compositions?** Longer, more varied pieces with extreme parameter settings might shift classification.
- **Is zone 6 inherently central?** The pentatonic scale and acoustic properties may naturally lead to zone 6 as the "average" zone.
- **How does rhythm affect classification?** All test pieces had similar BPM (125), which could be a strong classifier signal.
