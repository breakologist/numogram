# Origins of the Cthulhu Club

Nick Land's fictional correspondence (1949) between Captain Peter Vysparov and Dr. Echidna Stillwell — the origin story of the CCRU's intellectual lineage. Documents the founding of the Cthulhu Club, the coinage of "hyperstition," and the identification of Cthulhu with [[daniel-barker|Cthelll]].

[Source: LAND -- Origins of the Cthulhu Club.pdf]

## Vysparov's Sorcerous War (1944)

Captain Peter Vysparov, deployed to Dibboma (Eastern Sumatra) for psychological operations against the Japanese, used Dibboma witchcraft as "occult ammunition":

> "In just two weeks — between March 15th — 29th 1944 — three consecutive Japanese commanders were incapacitated by severe mental break-down. From leadership dysfunction, through violent assaults on subordinate personnel, to berserk derangement and paranoid ravings, culminating in suicide."

The weapon: "Oddubbite trances" performed by a Dibbomese witch. Vysparov witnessed "this meticulously deliberated descent into the splintering of self — complete personality disintegration — which she somehow traversed, and which she called shattering the mirror of existence."

### Oddubb = The Broken Mirror

This directly connects to [[numogram|Pandemonium Matrix]] demon **Oddubb** (Mesh-23, 7::2):

> "Mesh-23. Oddubb (Odba). Broken Mirror. Pitch Null. Net-Span 7::2. Syzygetic Chronodemon of Swamp-Labyrinths (and blind-doubles). Feeds Hold-Current. Rt-0:[X] Time loops, glamour and glosses."

The Dibbomese witch's "shattering the mirror of existence" IS Oddubb's domain — the Broken Mirror, time loops, glamour and glosses. The sorcery weapon was Oddubb-trance.

## The Coinage of Hyperstition

> "We are interested in fiction only insofar as it is simultaneously hyperstition — a term we have coined for semiotic productions that make themselves real — cryptic communications from the Old Ones, signalling return: shleth hud dopesh."

The word "hyperstition" is coined IN THIS TEXT — as a description of Lovecraft's fictions' relationship to the Old Ones. "Who writes, and who is written?" The Necronomicon is "of this kind."

## Cthulhu = Cthelll

Stillwell's identification:

> "My researches have led me to associate this Chthonian entity with the deep terrestrial intelligence inherent in the electromagnetic cauldron of the inner earth, in all of its intense reality, raw potentiality, and danger. According to the Nma she is the plane of Unlife, a veritable Cthelll."

> "Cthulhu's rising — like that of Kundalini as it was once understood — is a drawing down and under, a restoration of contact with abysmal intensities. Why would Cthulhu ever surface? She does not need rescuing, for she has her own line of escape, trajected through profundity."

Cthulhu does not rise from the sea — Cthulhu draws you DOWN. The "rising" is a descent. This inverts the standard Lovecraftian reading: the horror is not that something comes up from below, but that you are pulled down into it.

## The Vault of Murmurs

> "The Mu-Nagwi or dream-witches often claimed to meet these back-travellers in the Vault of Murmurs, where they would learn about future times. They said, however, that this time is compressing, and soon ends."

The Vault of Murmurs connects to [[demon-djynxx|Mur Mur]] (Mesh-29, 8::1) — the Dream-Serpent, "The Deep Ones." The Vault is Mur Mur's domain: oceanic sensation, gilled-unlife, spinal regressions. Dream-witches enter it to receive messages from future time.

## The Dibbomese Method

> "Dibbomese sorcery does not seem to be at all interested in judgements as to truth or falsity. It appears rather to estimate in each case the potential to make real, saying typically 'perhaps it can become so'..."

**shleth hud dopesh** — "perhaps it can become so." This is the operative phrase of hyperstition. Not "it is true" or "it will be true" but "it can become so." The Dibbomese do not assert — they evaluate potential for self-realization.

This phrase appears again in the Lemurian Times introductory material: "Rely on the lemurs. You will come to learn what that entails, shleth hud dopesh."

## The Lovecraft Connection

> "Lovecraft had a peculiar obsession with the South-Seas... I found that this topic quickly punctured his thin-crust of supercilious New-England rationalism, exposing an undercurrent of heavily fetishized archaic terror mixed with extreme racial paranoia."

Despite Lovecraft's racism, Stillwell considers his fictions "documents of the greatest importance" — because they are hyperstitional: "communications from the Old Ones, signalling return."

The Spring Equinoctial period (mid-March) — Lovecraft's intense-zone in "The Call of Cthulhu" — "comprises the intense-zone of Nma time-ritual." The dates of Vysparov's sorcerous war (March 15-29, 1944) fall exactly in this period.
