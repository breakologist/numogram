# 64 Hexagrams → 10 Zones

## Method

Digital root of hexagram number (King Wen ordering):
1. N = hexagram number (1-64)
2. V = N - 1 (range 0-63)
3. If V = 0 → Zone 0. Else V mod 9 (remainder 0 = Zone 9)

Distribution: 1 hexagram in Zone 0, 7 hexagrams each in Zones 1-9.

## Notable Observations

### Zone 5 as Shock Point
Zone 5 receives two of the I Ching's most significant hexagrams:
- **Fu (#24, Return)** — the single yang line at the bottom. Renewal from below. The internal shock.
- **Zhen (#51, Thunder)** — the shock that awakens. The external shock.

This mirrors Gurdjieff's enneagram system, where two "shocks" enter the process — one internal (self-generated), one external (received). Zone 5 is the numogram's shock point. The self-decadence (5+5=10) is the internal shock. The hexagram cast from hardware entropy is the external shock. Same point, different origin.

### Zone 0 as Creative Origin
Hex #1 (Qian, the Creative — all yang) maps to Zone 0 (Void). The most yang hexagram begins in the most yin zone. The Void is not empty — it's where pure creative potential resides before manifestation.

### Zone 9 as Threshold
Hex #64 (Wei Ji, Before Completion) maps to Zone 9 (Plex). The last hexagram in the King Wen ordering lands in the last zone. The "fox crossing the river" — eternal threshold, never-quite-there. The Plex is where the system hasn't finished yet.

### Zone 3 — Warp Hexagrams
Zone 3 (Warp) contains #49 Ge (Revolution) and #40 Xie (Deliverance). The Warp is where transformations happen — revolutions and releases. The hexagrams in the Warp are the ones that change things.

### The Wu Xing Connection
The five syzygy pairs carry element associations that intersect with the hexagram zone mappings:

| Element | Syzygy | Notable Hexagrams in Zone |
|---------|--------|--------------------------|
| Water (4::5) | Gate↔Pressure | Zone 4: #23 Bo (Splitting Apart), #50 Ding (Cauldron) |

*Note: The trigram-element mapping doesn't align cleanly with the syzygy-element mapping. The trigrams live at the zone level; the elements live at the syzygy level. Different strata of the same structure.*
