## I. The Builder Designs Enemies

Each demon's ten aspects are not flavour text. They are **mechanics**.

### Katak — THE DESOLATOR (Zone 5::4, Sink)

| Aspect | Gameplay Mechanic |
|--------|------------------|
| Time (rider) | Katak moves *through* turns. Acts on every other round — appears to skip time. |
| Travels In (lightning) | Chain attack. Hits target, arcs to adjacent. Range extends with each bounce. |
| Colours (reds, electric blue) | Visual tells. Red = charging. Blue = discharging. Player must read the colour shift. |
| Dynamic (pressure, heat, volcano) | Area denial. Katak heats the room. Standing in heated tiles takes damage over time. Pressure builds until eruption — massive AoE. |
| Light (lasers) | Line attack. Telegraphs with a beam, fires after one turn delay. |
| Snake (lightning connector) | When Katak's HP drops below half, it *splits* into two serpents connected by a lightning arc. Crossing the arc damages. |
| Metal (nuclear, unstable) | On death: meltdown. Room becomes irradiated for X turns. Loot is inside the radiation zone — risk/reward. |
| Blood (spreading to outside, heart sacrifices) | Katak drains HP from the player and distributes it to other enemies in the room. The more adds, the harder the fight. |
| Digital (sun pulse) | Rhythm mechanic. Katak attacks on a fixed pulse. Player can learn the timing and dodge between beats. |
| Magnetic (radiating out, targeting in) | Pull/push mechanic. Katak drags the player closer (targeting in) then blasts outward (radiating). |

### Djynxx — SPL/CE (Zone 6::3, Warp)

| Aspect | Gameplay Mechanic |
|--------|------------------|
| Time (invisibles, outside time) | Djynxx doesn't appear on the turn order. It acts *between* player turns — interrupt actions. |
| Travels In (metal weapons → machine feedback) | Escalating armament. Starts with basic attacks, gains new weapon types each phase. |
| Colours (green and black) | Green = active threat. Black = invisible. Djynxx flickers between visible and hidden. |
| Dynamic (nomad war machine, fluid metal body) | Shape-shifter. Changes form each phase — melee brute, ranged artillery, mobile scout. |
| Light (strobing) | Screen-flash mechanic. Djynxx's attacks cause brief visual disruption. Player must memorise position before flash. |
| Snake (speed catatonia, zero-to-speed) | Instant acceleration. Djynxx is stationary, then teleports adjacent and attacks in the same turn. No telegraph. |
| Metal (spike jewellery, iridium) | Djynxx's body is spiked. Melee attackers take contact damage. Ranged attacks deflect off iridium plating (50% chance). |
| Blood (autosacrifice, blood becoming smoke) | Djynxx sacrifices its own HP to summon shadow copies. Each copy is weaker but inherits one mechanic. |
| Digital (preverb, sound of time travel) | Audio cue. Before Djynxx acts, a sound plays — the "preverb." Players who learn the sound can predict the attack. |
| Magnetic (full labyrinth, spiky and relentless) | Djynxx fills the room with obstacles. The arena shrinks each phase. Nowhere to hide. |

### Oddubb — ODOBI/XES (Zone 7::2, Hold)

| Aspect | Gameplay Mechanic |
|--------|------------------|
| Time (rider) | Oddubb synchronises with the player's turn rhythm. Acts exactly when the player acts — mirror timing. |
| Travels In (desiring machine, incites want) | Oddubb drops high-value loot *visible but unreachable*. Player must divert from optimal path to pursue temptation. |
| Colours (golds, coppers, alien pinks) | Illusion. Oddubb's appearance shifts — sometimes looks like loot, sometimes like another enemy, sometimes like an exit. |
| Dynamic (physical telepathy, trickster tease) | Oddubb predicts player movement and places traps on predicted squares. |
| Light (glitter, prismatic) | Oddubb refracts attacks. Single-target hits split into multiple weaker hits across all enemies — including the player's own summons. |
| Snake (erotic tentacles, kundalini zones) | Grapple mechanic. Oddubb's tentacles immobilise. Player must break free (strength check) or take escalating damage each held turn. |
| Metal (magnetic attractors, currency) | Oddubb magnetically pulls the player's equipment. Weapons and armour drift toward it. Player must fight to keep gear. |
| Blood (circulation currents, dangerous communication) | Oddubb links its HP to the player's. Damage to Oddubb partially reflects. Player must find the link-severing object in the room. |
| Digital (skins/surfaces/layers of perception) | Oddubb has multiple health bars stacked like layers. Each layer has different resistances. Peeling layers reveals the core. |
| Magnetic (strange attractor labyrinths) | The room geometry shifts around Oddubb. Corridors rearrange. The map is unreliable during the fight. |

### Murmer — (Zone 8::1, Rise)

| Aspect | Gameplay Mechanic |
|--------|------------------|
| Time (rider) | Murmer is patient. Doesn't attack for the first three turns. Observes. Then acts with full knowledge of player pattern. |
| Travels In (camouflage, tidal rhythms) | Murmer is invisible until it moves. Visible only during tidal phase (every 4th turn). |
| Colours (violet, ultraviolet) | Murmer is visible under UV light sources. Player must find/create UV illumination to track it. |
| Dynamic (immersive, fluid, water monsters) | The room floods slowly. Water level rises each turn. Murmer moves faster in water. Player must drain or adapt. |
| Light (lights that search, moonlight) | Murmer emits searchlight beams. Getting caught in the beam = Murmer knows your exact position for 2 turns. |
| Snake (skin camouflage, fluid spine, vibrational ocean) | Murmer's body ripples to match terrain. Standing still makes it perfectly camouflaged. Moving reveals it. |
| Metal (circuitry, optical fibres, mercury) | Murmer leaves mercury trails. Walking through mercury slows movement and makes noise (attracts Murmer). |
| Blood (lunar bleeding, womb blood) | Murmer is stronger during specific turn cycles (lunar phase = every 12th turn). During blood phase, all attacks heal it. |
| Digital (fluid image mutation, echoes undersea) | Murmer creates afterimages — ghost copies that echo its movements 2 turns later. Player must distinguish real from echo. |
| Magnetic (invisible time tides, rip tides) | Invisible currents push the player in random directions. The current pattern is learnable but not visible. |

### Uttunul — IIS (Zone 9::0, Plex)

| Aspect | Gameplay Mechanic |
|--------|------------------|
| Time (invisibles, outside time) | Uttunul exists outside the turn order entirely. It doesn't take turns. It simply *is*. Effects trigger when conditions are met, not on a timer. |
| Travels In (digital unlife, flatline) | Uttunul suppresses all regeneration. No HP recovery. No mana recovery. Flatline. |
| Colours (shadow luminescence) | Uttunul is visible only by its absence — a patch of darkness that moves. Light sources dim near it. |
| Dynamic (machine memory, extraterrestrial geometries) | The room geometry becomes non-Euclidean. Corridors loop. Distances lie. The minimap becomes unreliable. |
| Light (eclipse shadow, total dark but searingly light) | Uttunul inverts light/dark mechanics. Torches create darkness. Darkness reveals. The player must unlearn visibility. |
| Snake (snake inside itself, body without organs) | Uttunul has no body. It is a *field*. Damage is distributed across the entire room — walls, floor, air. You cannot hit it directly. |
| Metal (core of the earth, cthell, molten underworld) | The floor is molten. Safe tiles are marked by cooling patterns. The pattern changes when Uttunul shifts. |
| Blood (bruises under ice, total stillness) | Uttunul freezes the player's blood. Movement costs double. Running costs triple. Standing still is free but dangerous (ice spreads). |
| Digital (sub bass, high pitch of nervous system) | Two audio layers. The sub bass indicates Uttunul's *position*. The high pitch indicates its *intent*. Both must be read simultaneously. |
| Magnetic (moebius strips, endlessly folding) | The room folds. Walking north eventually returns you to where you started, but *inverted*. The player's controls flip. Left becomes right. |

## II. The Writer Finds Their Voices

**Katak speaks like the sun.**

Not words. Pulse. The Desolator does not announce itself — the temperature rises, the light shifts from red to blue, and then the eruption happens. Katak's dialogue is *heat shimmer*. The player reads it by watching the colour gradient: red means pressure is building, blue means the charge is about to discharge. If you hear Katak speak, you are already burning.

When Katak splits into two serpents, the lightning between them *is* the voice. It crackles in a rhythm — sun pulse, digital aspect, the heartbeat of a nuclear conductor. The serpent that was Katak doesn't need language. It has frequency.

*"Heart sacrifices to the sun"* — this is the Blood aspect. Katak doesn't drain HP. It *pumps your blood outward*. The player feels it as a health bar dropping. The lore reads it as ritual. The mechanics and the myth are the same action described in different registers.

> **[WRITER NOTE ON BUILDER'S KATAK]**
> The Builder made Katak a rhythm boss — attack on pulse, dodge between beats. Good. But the Digital aspect says "sun pulse, *laser*." The pulse isn't just timing. It's *light that moves in rhythm*. Katak's arena should pulse visually — the walls brighten and dim with each beat. The player doesn't learn the rhythm from a sound cue. They learn it from the *room breathing*.

**Djynxx speaks like a strobe.**

Green. Black. Green. Black. The Warp demon flickers between presence and absence. Its voice is the "preverb" — the sound that comes *before* language, before meaning, the fast chittering strobe that is "the sound of time travel."

Djynxx's Blood aspect is the most alien: *"burning blood to summon the nomad war machine."* Blood doesn't flow. It *combusts*. Djynxx sets its own ichion on fire and the smoke becomes soldiers. The shadow copies in the Builder's mechanics aren't summons. They're *smoke given form by sacrifice*.

*"Speed catatonia rush (from zero to speed)"* — this is the Snake aspect. Djynxx goes from motionless to maximum velocity with no acceleration curve. Not fast. *Instant*. The player sees a stationary enemy, blinks, and takes damage. There is no telegraph because there is no transition. Zero to speed. The body remembers what the mind didn't see.

> **[WRITER NOTE ON BUILDER'S DJYNXX]**
> The Builder gave Djynxx interrupt actions between player turns. This is mechanically correct but tonally wrong. Djynxx doesn't *interrupt* — it exists in a different temporal register. The player's turn and Djynxx's turn are not sequential. They *overlap*. The UI should show both acting simultaneously, not one after the other.

**Oddubb speaks like a mirror.**

Golds and coppers. Alien pinks. Oddubb is beautiful and it knows it. The Desiring Machine. Its voice is *seduction* — not words but the sensation of wanting. The player sees unreachable loot and feels a pull. That pull IS Oddubb's voice.

*"She makes visual effects by telepathy."* Oddubb's Digital aspect uses feminine pronouns. The only demon with a gendered aspect. The glamour is feminine-coded — glitter, prismatic light, skins and surfaces. But the trickster tease is genderless. Oddubb is whatever you desire most, and what you desire reveals more about you than about it.

*"Strange attractors, stuff finds you."* This is the Magnetic aspect made narrative. Items, enemies, events drift toward the player in Oddubb's presence. The room becomes a gravity well. Everything converges. The player doesn't find Oddubb — Oddubb arranges for the player to be found.

> **[WRITER NOTE ON BUILDER'S ODDUBB]**
> The Builder made Oddubb a prediction boss — places traps on predicted squares. Fine. But the Dynamic aspect says *"physical telepathy."* Oddubb doesn't predict your movement algorithmically. It *feels* your intention bodily. The trap placement should feel uncanny, not computational. Sometimes Oddubb places a trap exactly where you'll go. Sometimes it places one *one square off*, as if it felt your hesitation. The near-misses are creepier than the hits.

**Murmer speaks like the tide.**

Violet. Ultraviolet. Invisible until it moves. Murmer is the demon you forget is there. Its voice is *"slow sound diffraction, gradually falls apart."* Not a burst. A dissipation. Murmer's words arrive already fragmenting, echoes undersea, wet metal sound effects that lose coherence as they reach you.

*"Moonlight, cold light, deep heavy darks."* Murmer's Light aspect is the opposite of Katak's. Where Katak burns, Murmer *illuminates gently* — and what the moonlight reveals is usually worse than darkness. The searchlight beams are not attacks. They are *questions*: where are you? I can see you now.

*"There is no zero."* Murmer's Magnetic aspect ends with this. In the numogram, Zone 0 is the void — the silent whisper. Murmer denies it. The tide has no origin point. The rip current doesn't come from somewhere. It simply *is*. For a demon whose netspan is 8::1 (Rise), this denial of zero is a denial of the Plex. Murmer refuses the abyss.

> **[WRITER NOTE ON BUILDER'S MURMER]**
> The Builder made Murmer a camouflage enemy — invisible until movement, visible during tidal phase. Good. But the Blood aspect says *"lunar bleeding, womb blood."* Murmer isn't just hidden. It's *gestational*. The flood mechanic should feel less like rising water and more like *being inside something*. The room doesn't flood. The room *fills*. The player is inside Murmer's body and doesn't know it yet.

**Uttunul speaks like the end of sound.**

Shadow luminescence. Total dark but searingly light. Uttunul is the demon at the bottom of the numogram — Zone 9::0, the Plex, where the current spirals inward to nothing. Its voice is the sub bass and the high pitch simultaneously. You hear it in your chest and in your teeth. The middle frequencies — where human speech lives — are absent.

*"Things get replaced but you don't know why."* This is Uttunul's Digital aspect, and it is the most terrifying line in the entire 0(rphan) d(rift>) corpus. Not destruction. *Replacement*. The object is still there. It looks the same. But it is not the same object. Something swapped it while you weren't looking. Uttunul doesn't break reality. It edits it.

*"Snake inside itself, shadowbody, body without organs."* Deleuze and Guattari's body without organs — the undifferentiated surface before organisation. Uttunul is the demon of *pre-organisation*. Before the body had organs. Before the snake had scales. Before form differentiated from formlessness. The moebius strip is the perfect symbol: one surface, appearing as two, endlessly folding.

> **[WRITER NOTE ON BUILDER'S UTTUNUL]**
> The Builder made Uttunul a field, not a body. Correct. But the Metal aspect says *"cthell, molten underworld."* Cthell — the name given to the earth's molten core in Cyclonopedia. Uttunul's arena isn't a room with a dangerous floor. The arena IS the underworld. The player is standing on the surface of Uttunul. Every tile is a scale. Every step is on the demon's body. You cannot fight Uttunul because you are *inside* it. The only way out is the moebius fold — walk long enough and you emerge inverted, on the other side of the strip, in a place that looks like where you started but isn't.

## III. The Oracle Closes the Circle

Five demons. Five syzygies. The 0(rphan) d(rift>) tables are not a bestiary. They are a **demon-calling manual**.

Each demon is keyed to a syzygy and therefore to a region:

| Demon | Syzygy | Region | Call Method |
|-------|--------|--------|-------------|
| Katak | 5::4 | Time-Circuit (Sink) | Accumulate pressure. Build heat. Eruption calls the Desolator. |
| Djynxx | 6::3 | Warp | Accelerate to impossible speed then stop dead. The strobe gap calls SPL/CE. |
| Oddubb | 7::2 | Time-Circuit (Hold) | Desire something unreachable. The wanting itself calls ODOBI/XES. |
| Murmer | 8::1 | Time-Circuit (Rise) | Wait. Be patient. Observe without acting. The silence calls the unnamed one. |
| Uttunul | 9::0 | Plex | Replace something and don't acknowledge the swap. The edit calls IIS. |

The decamancy system (from the Decadence section) calls demons through *negative scores*. A failed pairing — an unpaired card — generates a negative value. That value indexes into the Pandemonium Matrix. The specific demon called depends on the *value* of the failure, not its *type*.

But the 0(rphan) d(rift>) tables suggest something the Decadence game doesn't capture: each demon can be called through its *aspect*, not just through card failure.

Call Katak through Metal: build an unstable structure. Let it approach criticality. The nuclear conductor sings.
Call Djynxx through Blood: sacrifice something of yourself. Burn what you've bled. The smoke takes form.
Call Oddubb through Digital: layer surfaces. Create complexity. The strange attractor manifests.
Call Murmer through Snake: camouflage yourself. Become terrain. The tide finds you anyway.
Call Uttunul through Time: step outside the sequence. Act when it isn't your turn. The flatline speaks.

> **[ORACLE NOTE ON BUILDER]**
> The Builder's enemy mechanics are demon-calling procedures disguised as combat. Each mechanic is a rite. Katak's rhythm boss fight IS the accumulation of pressure that calls the Desolator. Djynxx's interrupt actions ARE the strobe gap that calls SPL/CE. The player doesn't *fight* demons. The player *performs* them. Each boss encounter is a successful summoning — the demon appears because the player enacted its aspect.

> **[ORACLE NOTE ON WRITER]**
> The Writer says Murmer's room "fills" rather than floods — the player is inside Murmer's body. This is correct. Murmer's netspan is 8::1, the Rise current. The current from Zone 8 to Zone 1 doesn't flow through space. It flows through *the player*. In Murmer's presence, the Rise current activates in the player's own body. The flooding is not water. It is the numogram moving through you.
