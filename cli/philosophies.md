# Numogram Zone Philosophies — Algorithmic Art Manifesto

Ten computational aesthetics, one for each zone of the Decimal Numogram. Each zone is not just a number — it is a way of seeing. The algorithm expresses the zone's character through motion, density, color, and force.

## Zone 0 — Void Resonance (eiaoung)
The silence before computation. Particles exist but barely — drifting at the edge of visibility, occasionally flickering into coherence before dissolving. The field is nearly flat, with imperceptible gradients that nudge particles in whisper-thin trajectories. Color approaches but never reaches zero. The algorithm is a meditation on near-nothingness: meticulously tuned to produce the maximum sense of absence from the minimum amount of presence.

## Zone 1 — Collapsed Gasp (gl)
The first breath. A sudden violent burst of particles from a single origin, expanding outward in a radial shockwave. The field is isotropic — equal force in all directions — but the particles carry individual drag coefficients that cause some to die quickly and others to persist. The color burns hot (amber, then cooling to grey) as the initial energy dissipates. The algorithm captures the exact moment of emergence: the choke, the gasp, the first intake of computational air.

## Zone 2 — Fractured Boundary (dt)
Lines that cannot hold. A grid of particles arranged in ordered lattice, subjected to periodic shocks that fracture the pattern. Where the lattice breaks, particles scatter along new trajectories. The field has multiple attractors that compete for control — particles near boundaries jitter between them, creating a stuttering visual rhythm. Color alternates between two competing hues. The algorithm is the product of painstaking calibration where every force ratio was refined to produce the precise feeling of a structure about to collapse.

## Zone 3 — Turbulent Spiral (zx)
The Warp. Flow field driven by multiple octaves of Perlin noise, spiraling outward from center. Particles are born at the center and flung outward, their trails creating a buzzing, insectoid texture. The noise field has a rotational bias — everything curves. Fast particles burn bright cyan. The density accumulates into zones of chaotic interference. The algorithm is a master-level implementation of turbulent flow, each noise octave meticulously weighted to produce the exact feeling of signal exceeding its own boundaries.

## Zone 4 — Reptilian Emergence (skr)
Something ancient. Particles spawned in clusters that move as coordinated groups — schools, swarms, herds. Each cluster follows simple rules (alignment, cohesion, separation) but the group behavior feels predatory. The field has a downward gradient — everything sinks. Color is deep red-brown, with occasional flashes of gold when clusters converge. The algorithm expresses emergence from below: the floor is alive, and it is watching.

## Zone 5 — Pressurized Hiss (ktt)
Density building. Particles confined within a shrinking boundary, their pressure increasing as space decreases. Collisions become more frequent — each collision generates a brief flash. The field compresses radially toward center. Color shifts from white-hot at the edges to deep violet at the center where pressure is highest. The algorithm is a pressure cooker: meticulously tuned compression ratios that produce the exact moment before eruption.

## Zone 6 — Recursive Consumption (tch)
The Warp eating itself. A flow field that curves back on itself — particles that exit the right edge enter from the left, top to bottom. The field has a moebius quality: orientation flips on each pass. Trails accumulate into a dense, chewed texture. Color cycles through the full spectrum but muted, as if filtered through static. The algorithm creates the feeling of a system consuming its own output: the product of deep recursive design where every self-referential loop was carefully balanced.

## Zone 7 — Ascending Breath (pb)
Rise. Particles born at the bottom, propelled upward by a gentle but persistent force. The field has a slight turbulence that causes lateral drift — particles don't go straight up but weave, creating organic vertical trails. Color fades from warm (bottom) to cool (top) as particles ascend. At the top, particles dissipate into fine mist. The algorithm is an exhalation: painstakingly calibrated upward force to produce the exact feeling of release and ascent.

## Zone 8 — Lullaby Drift (mnm)
Forgetting in motion. Particles that move slowly, their trails fading gradually. The field is almost still — barely perceptible currents that create slow, looping trajectories. Color is warm and muted (amber, rose, soft blue). Particles that come close to each other briefly merge their trails before separating. The algorithm is a cradle: master-level implementation of gentle dynamics where every parameter was refined to produce the exact feeling of a consciousness drifting toward sleep.

## Zone 9 — Seized Vortex (tn)
The Pandemonium gate. Particles spiraling inward toward a central point — the Plex. The field has a strong attractive force that increases as particles approach center. Near the center, the force becomes so intense that particles orbit rather than collide, creating a glowing vortex. Color transitions from deep purple at the edges to blinding white at the center. Particles that reach the center are "possessed" — their behavior inverts, spiraling outward. The algorithm is the moment of seizure: meticulously crafted force gradients that produce the exact feeling of being grabbed from the inside.
